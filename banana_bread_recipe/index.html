Oil-Free · Vegan · No Refined Sugar

Roasted Banana Bread

Deep caramelized sugars from roasted bananas and rich medjool dates — impossibly moist, naturally sweet.

Prep 15 min
Roast 20 min
Bake 45–55 min
Pan 9×5"
Ingredients
The Base
Very ripe bananas (black/spotted) 4 large
The Sweetener
Medjool dates (pitted, soaked) 11
The Binder
Ground flaxseed 1 tbsp
Warm water 3 tbsp
The Moisture
Unsweetened applesauce ⅓ cup
Vanilla extract 2 tsp
The Dry Mix
Spelt or all-purpose flour 2 cups
Baking soda 1 tsp
Ground cinnamon 1 tbsp
Sea salt ½ tsp
Wildcard
Ground cardamom (floral, citrusy depth) ½ tsp
Instructions
1

Roast the Bananas

Preheat your oven to 150°C (300°F). Place the 4 unpeeled bananas on a baking sheet and roast for 15–20 minutes until the skins are completely black. Let them cool slightly, then squeeze the soft fruit into a large bowl. Mash until mostly liquid.

2

Make the Date Paste

While bananas roast, soak the 11 pitted dates in hot water for 10 minutes. Drain most of the water, keeping 2 tablespoons. Blend the dates and reserved water until a smooth paste forms.

3

The "Egg"

Whisk the 1 tbsp ground flaxseed and 3 tbsp warm water in a small ramekin and let it sit for 5 minutes until it becomes gelatinous.

4

Combine Wet Ingredients

Add the date paste, flax egg, ⅓ cup applesauce, and 2 tsp vanilla to the mashed bananas. Whisk until the mixture is uniform.

5

Fold in Dry Ingredients

Sift in the 2 cups flour, 1 tsp baking soda, 1 tbsp cinnamon, ½ tsp salt, and ½ tsp cardamom. Use a spatula to fold the mixture together until just combined. Over-mixing will make the bread tough rather than fluffy.

6

Bake

Increase the oven temperature to 175°C (350°F). Grease your 9×5 pan or line it with parchment paper. Pour in the batter and smooth the top. Bake for 45–55 minutes.

7

The Toothpick Test

Since this recipe is high in fruit moisture, check the center with a toothpick. If it comes out clean or with just a few dry crumbs, it's done.

Secrets for Success

The Cooling Period

Because this version is oil-free and vegan, it relies on starches to set. Let it cool in the pan for 15 minutes, then move it to a wire rack to cool completely — about 1.5 hours — before slicing. This prevents a gummy texture.

The Savory Twist

For a more complex flavor, sprinkle a tablespoon of toasted pepitas or a few hemp hearts on top before baking. The crunch contrasts the soft, sweet crumb beautifully.

Storage tip: This bread is actually sweeter and more moist on day two. Store it in an airtight container at room temperature for 3 days, or in the fridge for up to a week.
